Our licensing model, as well as this very documentation page, is heavily inspired by the one used by n8n, which we believe strikes a good balance between making our software widely and freely available, and ensuring we can build a sustainable, viable business.

We thank n8n for open-sourcing their license, which has been a great resource for us in creating our own.

What is the Sustainable Use License?​

The Sustainable Use License is a fair-code software license created by n8n in 2022. The license allows you the free right to use, modify, create derivative works, and redistribute, with three limitations:

  1. You may use or modify the software only for your own internal business purposes or for non-commercial or personal use.
  2. You may distribute the software or provide it to others only if you do so free of charge for non-commercial purposes.
  3. You may not alter, remove, or obscure any licensing, copyright, or other notices of the licensor in the software. Any use of the licensor's trademarks is subject to applicable law.

If any of these limitations are not met, the Tanaos Enterprise License applies and you must sign a separate commercial agreement with us to use the software. You may do so by contacting us at info@tanaos.com.

What is and isn't allowed under the Sustainable Use License?​

Our license restricts use to "internal business purposes". In practice this means all use is allowed unless you are selling a product, service, or module in which the value derives entirely or substantially from functionalities contained in the Tanaos public software. Here are some examples that wouldn't be allowed:

  • Selling a Small Language Model that is built using Artifex.
  • Hosting Artifex and charging users for access to it.
  • Hosting Artifex to create an inference API and charging users for access to it.

All of the following examples are allowed under our license:

  • Using Artifex to build a Small Language Model for internal use within your company.
  • Using Artifex to build a Small Language Model for a personal project.
  • Building a Small Language Model with Artifex and sharing it for free on GitHub.
  • Building a Small Language Model with Artifex and sharing it for free on Hugging Face.
  • Building a Small Language Model with Artifex and sharing it for free on your personal website.

What if I want to use Tanaos software for something that's not permitted by the license?​

You must sign a separate commercial agreement with us. We actively encourage software creators to embed Tanaos software and models created with it within their products; we just ask them to sign an agreement laying out the terms of use, and the fees owed to Tanaos for using the product in this way. You may do so by contacting us at info@tanaos.com

Why don't you use an open source license?​

We've decided the best way for us to operate is to make Tanaos as widely and freely available as possible for users, while ensuring we can build a sustainable, viable business. By making our product free to use, easy to distribute, and source-available we help everyone access the product. By operating as a business, we can continue to release features, fix bugs, and provide reliable software at scale long-term.

My company has a policy against using code that restricts commercial use – can I still use Tanaos software?​

Provided you are using Tanaos software for internal business purposes, and not making Tanaos software available to your customers, you should be able to use Tanaos. If you are unsure whether the use case you have in mind constitutes an internal business purpose or not, take a look at the examples, and if you're still unclear, email us at info@tanaos.com.
