## What is the Sustainable Use License?

The Sustainable Use License is a fair-code software license created by [n8n](https://n8n.io) in 
2022. The license allows you the free right to use, modify, create derivative works, and 
redistribute, with three limitations:

1. You may use or modify the software only for your own internal business purposes or for 
non-commercial or personal use.
2. You may distribute the software or provide it to others only if you do so free of charge for 
non-commercial purposes.
3. You may not alter, remove, or obscure any licensing, copyright, or other notices of the licensor 
in the software. Any use of the licensor's trademarks is subject to applicable law.

If any of these limitations are not met, the [Tanaos Enterprise License](/tanaos-enterprise-license) 
applies and you must sign a separate commercial agreement with us to use the software. You may
do so by contacting us at <a href={`mailto:${CONTACT_EMAIL}`}>{CONTACT_EMAIL}</a>.

## What is and isn't allowed under the Sustainable Use License?

Our license restricts use to "internal business purposes". In practice this means all use is 
allowed unless you are selling a product, service, or module in which the value derives entirely 
or substantially from functionalities contained in the Tanaos public software. Here are some 
examples that wouldn't be allowed:

- Selling a Small Language Model that is built using Artifex.
- Hosting Artifex and charging users for access to it.
- Hosting Artifex to create an inference API and charging users for access to it.

All of the following examples are allowed under our license:

- Using Artifex to build a Small Language Model for internal use within your company.
- Using Artifex to build a Small Language Model for a personal project.
- Building a Small Language Model with Artifex and sharing it for free on GitHub.
- Building a Small Language Model with Artifex and sharing it for free on Hugging Face.
- Building a Small Language Model with Artifex and sharing it for free on your personal website.

## Why don't you use an open source license?

We've decided the best way for us to operate is to make Tanaos as widely and freely available as 
possible for users, while ensuring we can build a sustainable, viable business. By making our 
product free to use, easy to distribute, and source-available we help everyone access the product. 
By operating as a business, we can continue to release features, fix bugs, and provide reliable 
software at scale long-term.

software available to your customers, you should be able to use Tanaos. If you are unsure whether 
the use case you have in mind constitutes an internal business purpose or not, take a look at the
